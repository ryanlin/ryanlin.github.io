Product

NextGen @ Roblox

Having made many memories on Roblox as a kid, I was excited to hop on board when asked to join their unique accelerator internship program . With my team of 4, we pitched and developed NextGen, a cyberpunk experience stretching the platform's capabilities. Throughout this project, I worked closely with a variety of teams at Roblox as well as with both players and developers, uncovering and presenting valuable insights on both user types and the platform to the company.

MCAV @ UNR

As robotics expands into more and more facets of our world, it is the optimal time to create tools that aid the research, development, and education around this bourgeoning field. Multisensor Calibration And Visualizer is a React + Electron app I designed and developed with a team of 4 based on a found need for simpler calibration of sensors on an autonomous robot, such as a self-driving car. We continue to work closely with faculty at the University of Nevada - Reno in testing and improving the product.

Tech

Subterranean Artifact Detection

Working as an undergraduate researcher at the University of Nevada, Reno, I proposed and worked on a lightweight and realtime object recognition method for underground environments, utilizing classical computer vision techniques as well as the SVM machine learning model. Although it was ultimately not deployed (as can be the case with research), the method was explored as a potential solution for our Team CERBERUS in the DARPA Subterranean Challenge, who ultimately won 1st place.

COVID19 Analysis with Tensorflow

In the last quarter of 2020, data around COVID-19 became large enough to form a clearer picture. As my final project for UNR's Big Data course, my team and I acquired the CSSE COVID-19 Dataset compiled by John Hopkins University and made 3 contributions upon it:
1. Converted the dataset to a TensorFlow-ready format using Pandas.
2. Performed basic stats analysis on the data.
3. Trained a sequential model in TF on the data with the goal of forecasting regional cases. 

Real-time MTG Card Recognition

Gaming and Streaming are two of the fastest growing industries in the world right now, and physical games are far from left behind in this trend. As part of a team for the 2020 DocuSign Hackathon @ Galvanize SF, I built a card detection and identification system for a small set of cards using segmentation, and basic mask subtraction. This system identified Magic The Gathering Cards in a match as the video stream came in so players can easily access information about the match they are viewing.

Revisiting the BERT Neural Network

BERT was a breakthrough Neural Network developed for language modeling by Google AI Language; however, like many neural networks, it suffered from being a black box model. In this project, my team attached probes to the attention heads of this model to try and clarify how the model worked. As the undergrad in this team of graduate researchers, I contributed by writing Python scripts for cleaning and parsing the corpus used for this natural language processing project.

Design

UnHynged

UnHynged is a uniquely collapseable tacklebox made for painters. I was able to broaden my understanding of the design thinking process by wading into a space I had 0 knowledge in (painting) and totally depend on empathizing with users to find what they need. The solution ideation process also stimulated me beyond my typical thought process, adamantly learning from my talented mechanical engineering partner as we designed, developed, tested, and presented a physical and original product.

Tissue Tags

Tissue Tags are visually novel tissue holders, whether it's a puppy holding one in its mouth or a volcano ejecting a tissue. I developed this concept for the 2017 Inneventure Fund Business Competition, becoming 1 of 4 winners and the only non-graduate to win a portion of the fund at 17yo. Seeking out mentorship, I learned to create a business plan, pitches, and projections using databases like Mintel and EDGAR. WIth my partner, we iterated on prototypes using Blender, 3D printing and a lot of messing up.
